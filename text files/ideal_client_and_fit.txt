==================================================
WHO WE SERVE BEST
==================================================

Matai Tech is designed specifically for operations-heavy small and growing businesses that are ready to scale but held back by manual processes.

We're not for everyone - and that's intentional. Our best clients share specific characteristics that make automation successful and ROI achievable.

==================================================
IDEAL CLIENT CHARACTERISTICS
==================================================

COMPANY SIZE
- Revenue: $500K - $10M annually
- Employees: 5-50 people
- Stage: Post-startup, entering growth phase
- Structure: Team-based with some specialization

Why this range?
- Below $500K: Often too early, processes not yet established, budget constraints
- Above $10M: Usually need enterprise solutions or have internal automation teams
- 5-50 employees: Large enough to have repetitive processes, small enough to move quickly

OPERATIONAL PROFILE
- Spending 10+ hours/week on repetitive manual tasks
- Using 5+ different SaaS tools or systems
- Processes are established and repeatable
- Pain points are clear and measurable
- Ready to invest in operational efficiency

TECHNOLOGY PROFILE
- Currently using SaaS tools (CRM, accounting, project management)
- Minimal or no custom automation in place
- Behind on technology adoption but ready to catch up
- Comfortable with cloud-based tools
- Not requiring legacy system integration (or willing to migrate)

MINDSET AND CULTURE
- Values reliability and consistency over cutting-edge innovation
- Willing to invest upfront for long-term efficiency
- Understands that automation amplifies processes (not redesigns them)
- Open to changing workflows if it improves automation
- Committed to seeing implementation through

DECISION-MAKING
- Single decision-maker or small decision committee
- Can make purchasing decisions within 1-2 weeks
- Willing to provide access and resources for implementation
- Available for weekly check-ins during implementation

GOOD FIT INDICATORS
==================================================

STRONG INDICATORS (Green Light)

1. MEASURABLE PAIN
- "We spend 15 hours per week on manual data entry"
- "My team wastes 3 hours every Monday creating reports"
- "We lose deals because leads fall through the cracks"
- Can quantify time or money wasted

2. PROCESS MATURITY
- Processes are documented or consistently repeated
- Team members do the same task the same way
- Clear workflow with defined steps
- Not constantly changing how things work

3. READY TO INVEST
- Understands ROI and payback period
- Budget allocated or can be approved within 2 weeks
- Willing to invest in long-term efficiency
- Values quality over cheapest option

4. COMMITTED TO IMPLEMENTATION
- Can dedicate time for discovery and implementation
- Willing to provide access to systems
- Available for weekly check-ins
- Team members can participate in training

5. REALISTIC EXPECTATIONS
- Understands automation won't be perfect day 1
- Willing to iterate and refine
- Knows some processes may need adjustment
- Not expecting magic or instant transformation

6. INTEGRATION FRIENDLY
- Using modern SaaS tools with APIs
- Comfortable with cloud-based solutions
- Willing to consider new tools if needed
- Not requiring complex legacy system integration

POSITIVE SIGNALS IN CONVERSATIONS:
- "How soon can we start?"
- "What do you need from us?"
- "We've been looking for this for months"
- "This is costing us $X per month currently"
- "We tried to do it ourselves but need help"

==================================================
POOR FIT INDICATORS
==================================================

RED FLAGS (Proceed with Caution or Decline)

1. UNDEFINED PROCESSES
- "We kind of just figure it out as we go"
- "Everyone does it differently"
- "It depends on who's doing it"
- No consistency or documentation

Why this is a problem: Automation amplifies existing processes. If the process isn't defined, automation will fail or encode inconsistency.

2. BUDGET CONSTRAINTS
- "Can you do this for under $500?"
- "We don't have budget right now"
- "We need to see results before paying anything"
- Looking for cheapest option regardless of quality

Why this is a problem: Quality automation requires investment. Cheap automation breaks and creates more work than it saves.

3. UNREALISTIC EXPECTATIONS
- "Can you automate our entire business?"
- "This should be really simple and quick, right?"
- "We need this done by end of week"
- "It should work perfectly with zero issues"

Why this is a problem: These expectations lead to disappointment regardless of quality of work delivered.

4. LACK OF COMMITMENT
- "We're talking to several other vendors"
- "We're not sure if this is a priority right now"
- "I'll need to get back to you about access"
- Can't commit to regular check-ins or meetings

Why this is a problem: Successful implementation requires collaboration and access. Without commitment, project will stall.

5. LEGACY SYSTEM CONSTRAINTS
- "We need to integrate with our custom AS/400 system from 1995"
- "Our main system doesn't have an API"
- "We can't change anything about our core system"
- Heavy reliance on outdated, closed systems

Why this is a problem: Integration with legacy systems requires expensive custom development, not our focus.

6. CONSTANT PROCESS CHANGES
- "We're redesigning this process next month"
- "We change how we do things all the time"
- "We're not sure what our process should be"
- Unstable operations or constant pivoting

Why this is a problem: Building automation for changing targets wastes money. Stabilize first, then automate.

7. REQUIRING 24/7 SUPPORT
- "We need someone available at 3am"
- "We can't have any downtime ever"
- "Mission-critical system that can never fail"
- Expecting enterprise-level SLA without enterprise budget

Why this is a problem: We provide business-hours support with 4-hour critical response. 24/7 requires enterprise pricing.

WARNING PHRASES:
- "Just a quick fix" (usually not quick)
- "Should be really simple" (rarely is)
- "Can't you just..." (implies unrealistic simplicity)
- "We need this yesterday" (rushed projects fail)
- "What's the cheapest way..." (prioritizes cost over value)

QUALIFICATION QUESTIONS
==================================================

Use these questions to assess fit during discovery calls:

PAIN AND URGENCY
1. "What manual task is currently costing you the most time?"
2. "How many hours per week does your team spend on this?"
3. "What's prompting you to look for automation now?"
4. "What happens if you don't solve this in the next 3-6 months?"

PROCESS MATURITY
5. "Walk me through how you currently do [task] from start to finish"
6. "Does everyone on your team do it the same way?"
7. "Do you have documentation or SOPs for this process?"
8. "How often does this process change?"

BUDGET AND INVESTMENT
9. "What's your budget range for solving this?"
10. "Have you allocated budget, or does it need approval?"
11. "What ROI would make this a no-brainer for you?"
12. "How do you typically evaluate investments like this?"

DECISION-MAKING
13. "Who else needs to be involved in this decision?"
14. "What's your typical decision timeline?"
15. "What would prevent you from moving forward?"

TECHNICAL ENVIRONMENT
16. "What tools and systems are you currently using?"
17. "Do these tools have APIs or integration capabilities?"
18. "Are you open to adding new tools if it makes automation easier?"
19. "Any legacy systems we need to work with?"

COMMITMENT
20. "What does your next 2-3 weeks look like availability-wise?"
21. "Can you commit to weekly 30-minute check-ins during implementation?"
22. "Who on your team can help us understand the detailed workflows?"

EXPECTATIONS
23. "What does success look like for this project?"
24. "What timeline are you hoping for?"
25. "Have you tried to automate this before? What happened?"

COMPANIES WE TYPICALLY DECLINE
==================================================

To set clear expectations:

STARTUPS (PRE-REVENUE OR VERY EARLY)
Why: Processes not yet established, budget constraints, too much change
Better option: Get to $500K revenue first, then automate

ENTERPRISE (50+ EMPLOYEES, $10M+ REVENUE)
Why: Need enterprise solutions, internal automation teams, our model doesn't scale here
Better option: Hire internal automation engineer or use enterprise consultancy

COMPANIES IN CONSTANT PIVOT MODE
Why: Automating changing processes wastes money
Better option: Stabilize operations first, then automate

BUDGET-DRIVEN BUYERS (CHEAPEST OPTION FOCUS)
Why: Quality automation requires proper investment
Better option: DIY with Zapier or hire offshore freelancer (if willing to accept the risks)

ONE-OFF "QUICK FIX" SEEKERS
Why: Not enough value for either party, not our business model
Better option: Freelancer or DIY

LEGACY SYSTEM HEAVY OPERATIONS
Why: Requires expensive custom development, not our focus
Better option: Custom software development firm
