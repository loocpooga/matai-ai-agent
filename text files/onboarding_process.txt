Matai Tech - Client Onboarding Process

==================================================
ONBOARDING OVERVIEW
==================================================

Our onboarding process is designed to get you from "signed agreement" to "first automation running" in 7-10 days.

The key principle: Get value into your hands quickly, then expand.

Timeline:
- Day 1-2: Setup and kickoff
- Day 3-7: Discovery and planning
- Day 7-10: First automation live
- Week 2-4: Additional automations and optimization

==================================================
WHAT HAPPENS AFTER YOU SIGN
==================================================

WITHIN 24 HOURS:
----------------------------------------------
You'll receive:

1. Welcome email with:
   - What to expect next
   - Calendar link to schedule kickoff call
   - Access to our client portal / shared workspace
   - Pre-kickoff questionnaire (helps us prepare)

2. Onboarding checklist:
   - Tasks you need to complete
   - Access and credentials to provide
   - Timeline and key dates
   - Contact information for your implementation team

3. Shared workspace setup:
   - Google Drive folder or Notion workspace
   - Where we'll collaborate and share documents
   - Where you can see progress in real-time

4. Invoice for setup fee:
   - Due before kickoff call
   - Payment options: Credit card, ACH, check
   - Once paid, we schedule kickoff

WITHIN 48-72 HOURS (After payment):
----------------------------------------------
1. Kickoff call scheduled:
   - 60-90 minutes
   - You, your key team members, our implementation team
   - Can be in-person or virtual

2. Pre-kickoff questionnaire reviewed:
   - Your answers help us prepare
   - Questions about processes, tools, goals
   - Any preparation we need to do

3. Tools and access preparation:
   - Create accounts if needed (Make, n8n, etc.)
   - Prepare credential checklist
   - Set up monitoring infrastructure

==================================================
WEEK 1: KICKOFF AND DISCOVERY
==================================================

DAY 1-2: KICKOFF CALL (60-90 minutes)
----------------------------------------------
Agenda:

1. Introductions (10 minutes):
   - Meet the implementation team
   - Understand roles and responsibilities
   - Set communication preferences

2. Process deep-dive (30-40 minutes):
   - Walk through manual processes step-by-step
   - Screen sharing to show actual workflows
   - Understand pain points and edge cases
   - Identify what success looks like

3. Technical review (15-20 minutes):
   - Review tools and systems you use
   - Understand access levels available
   - Identify any technical constraints
   - Discuss integration approach

4. Planning and prioritization (15-20 minutes):
   - Agree on which automation to build first
   - Set timeline expectations
   - Define success criteria
   - Identify quick wins

5. Next steps (10 minutes):
   - Access and credentials needed
   - Who we need to interview
   - Communication plan
   - Schedule follow-up check-ins

What to prepare:
- Be ready to screen share your current process
- Invite team members who do the manual work
- Have admin access to your tools available
- List of questions or concerns

Deliverables from kickoff:
- Process map (we document what you showed us)
- Prioritized automation roadmap
- Timeline with key milestones
- Access and credential checklist


DAY 2-4: ACCESS AND SETUP
----------------------------------------------
What we do:
- Set up automation platform (Make, n8n, Zapier)
- Create staging/test environment
- Begin connecting to your systems
- Set up monitoring and error alerts
- Create documentation framework

What you do:
- Provide credentials for integrations
- Grant necessary permissions
- Create service accounts if needed
- Approve any new tool purchases

How credentials are handled:
- We use encrypted password managers (1Password, Bitwarden)
- Service accounts preferred over personal credentials
- Minimum required permissions only
- Access is revocable anytime
- We sign NDA if required

Common tools we need access to:
- CRM (HubSpot, Salesforce, etc.)
- Accounting (QuickBooks, Xero, etc.)
- Email (Gmail, Outlook)
- Spreadsheets (Google Sheets, Airtable)
- Project management (Asana, Monday, etc.)
- E-commerce (Shopify, WooCommerce, etc.)

Time required from you: 30-60 minutes total


DAY 3-5: DISCOVERY INTERVIEWS (Optional)
----------------------------------------------
If needed, we'll interview team members who:
- Perform the manual tasks currently
- Will use the automations
- Have knowledge of edge cases and exceptions

Interview format:
- 30-minute video calls
- Walk through the process they do
- Discuss pain points and challenges
- Understand what "good" looks like

What we're looking for:
- Step-by-step process details
- Edge cases and exceptions
- Error scenarios and how they're handled
- Volume and frequency
- Pain points and frustrations

Deliverable:
- Detailed process documentation
- Edge case list
- Success criteria refined


DAY 5-7: PLANNING AND DESIGN
----------------------------------------------
What we do:
- Design automation architecture
- Plan error handling approach
- Identify data validation points
- Create test scenarios
- Design monitoring and alerts
- Document the approach

What we deliver:
- Automation design document
- Data flow diagram
- Test plan
- Timeline for implementation

Your role:
- Review and approve design
- Ask questions and provide feedback
- Flag any missed requirements
- Approve to proceed to build

Time required: 30-minute review call

==================================================
WEEK 2: BUILD AND TEST - FIRST AUTOMATION
==================================================

DAY 8-12: BUILDING
----------------------------------------------
What we do:
- Build automation in staging environment
- Implement error handling
- Set up monitoring and alerts
- Test with sample data
- Document how it works
- Prepare for your review

Daily updates:
- Async updates via Slack/Email
- Screenshots or videos of progress
- Flag any questions or blockers
- Keep you informed without meetings

What we need from you:
- Respond to questions (24-48 hour turnaround)
- Provide sample/test data if needed
- Review draft automation (15-30 min)

Typical question examples:
- "What should happen if customer has 2 email addresses?"
- "Should we create a new record or update existing?"
- "What priority should this notification be?"


DAY 12-14: YOUR REVIEW AND TESTING
----------------------------------------------
What happens:
1. Demo call (30 minutes):
   - We show you the automation running
   - Walk through each step
   - Demonstrate error handling
   - Show monitoring dashboard

2. Your testing period (24-48 hours):
   - We give you access to staging environment
   - You test with real scenarios
   - Provide feedback on any adjustments needed
   - Verify it meets your expectations

3. Adjustments (if needed):
   - We make any requested changes
   - Re-test together
   - Iterate until you're satisfied

Approval criteria:
- Automation completes successfully with real data
- Error handling works properly
- Results match your expectations
- You understand how to monitor it
- Ready to deploy to production


DAY 14: PRODUCTION DEPLOYMENT
----------------------------------------------
What happens:
1. Pre-deployment checklist:
   - Final testing complete
   - Monitoring configured
   - Error alerts set up
   - Documentation ready
   - Team notified

2. Deployment (usually takes minutes):
   - Enable automation in production
   - Monitor first few runs closely
   - Verify data flowing correctly
   - Confirm notifications working

3. Post-deployment monitoring:
   - We watch closely for first 24-48 hours
   - Quick response to any issues
   - Fine-tuning based on real data
   - Daily status updates

What you experience:
- Automation starts handling tasks automatically
- You receive notifications (if configured)
- Can view status in dashboard
- Manual process now automated

Time required from you: 15 minutes to verify first runs

==================================================
WEEK 2-4: ADDITIONAL AUTOMATIONS (If applicable)
==================================================

For Growth and Transformation packages with multiple automations:

The process repeats:
1. Design next automation
2. Build and test
3. Review and approve
4. Deploy to production

One automation at a time approach:
- Ensures quality and reliability
- Allows you to validate value incrementally
- Reduces risk
- Builds confidence

Timeline:
- Efficiency Package (1-2 automations): Week 2-3
- Growth Package (3-5 automations): Week 2-4
- Transformation Package (6+ automations): Week 2-8

==================================================
WEEK 3-4: TRAINING AND HANDOFF
==================================================

TRAINING SESSION (60-90 minutes)
----------------------------------------------
What we cover:

1. Automation overview (15 minutes):
   - What each automation does
   - How they work together
   - Business impact and ROI

2. Monitoring and status (20 minutes):
   - How to check if automations are running
   - Understanding the dashboard
   - What to look for
   - Common status indicators

3. What to do if issues occur (15 minutes):
   - How to identify problems
   - When to contact us vs. fix yourself
   - How to check logs
   - Emergency contact information

4. Making changes (15 minutes):
   - How to request changes
   - What you can modify yourself (if applicable)
   - What requires our help

5. Q&A and practice (20 minutes):
   - Answer all questions
   - Walk through scenarios
   - Practice checking status
   - Build confidence

Format:
- Live video call (recorded for reference)
- Screen sharing and demos
- Hands-on practice
- Interactive Q&A

Who should attend:
- Anyone who monitors the automations
- Team members who interact with automations
- IT/Operations leads
- Executives (optional, executive summary provided)


DOCUMENTATION PACKAGE
----------------------------------------------
What you receive:

1. Executive Summary (1-2 pages):
   - What we built
   - Expected ROI and time savings
   - How to measure success
   - Contact information

2. Automation Guides (per automation):
   - What it does (plain English)
   - How it works (technical details)
   - What triggers it
   - What to expect (timing, notifications)
   - Common scenarios

3. Monitoring Guide:
   - How to check status
   - Dashboard walkthrough
   - What to look for
   - When to be concerned

4. Troubleshooting Guide:
   - Common issues and solutions
   - How to check logs
   - When to contact support
   - Emergency procedures

5. Architecture Documentation:
   - Data flow diagrams
   - Integration details
   - Error handling logic
   - Technical specifications

6. Video Library:
   - Recorded training session
   - Walkthrough of each automation
   - How to use monitoring tools
   - FAQ responses

Format:
- Google Docs (editable, accessible)
- PDF versions (permanent record)
- Video hosted on Loom or YouTube (private)
- Organized in shared workspace


HANDOFF AND GO-LIVE
----------------------------------------------
Final checklist before considering onboarding complete:

□ All automations deployed and running in production
□ Team trained and comfortable with monitoring
□ Documentation reviewed and accessible
□ Contact information and support process clear
□ Monitoring dashboards set up and working
□ Error alerts configured and tested
□ First ROI metrics captured (baseline vs. current)
□ Any questions answered
□ Monthly support process explained
□ You're confident and ready

Sign-off meeting (30 minutes):
- Review accomplishments
- Confirm everything is working as expected
- Transition to ongoing support phase
- Answer final questions
- Celebrate the win!

==================================================
ONBOARDING SUCCESS FACTORS
==================================================

What makes onboarding successful:

CLIENT SIDE:
1. Timely responses to questions (24-48 hours)
2. Key stakeholders engaged and available
3. Access and credentials provided promptly
4. Open communication about concerns
5. Realistic expectations
6. Willingness to iterate and refine

OUR SIDE:
1. Clear communication and transparency
2. Deliver first automation quickly (week 1-2)
3. Proactive problem-solving
4. Quality and reliability focus
5. Comprehensive documentation
6. Patient training and support

COMMON CHALLENGES AND HOW WE HANDLE THEM:
----------------------------------------------
Challenge: Requirements change during discovery
Solution: Flexible scope, adjust plan, communicate impact

Challenge: Access delays (credentials, permissions)
Solution: Clear checklist upfront, follow up proactively

Challenge: Edge cases discovered during testing
Solution: Build time for iteration, handle edge cases properly

Challenge: Team resistance or hesitation
Solution: Involve team early, emphasize benefits, thorough training

Challenge: Technical limitations discovered
Solution: Honest assessment, propose workarounds, adjust scope if needed
